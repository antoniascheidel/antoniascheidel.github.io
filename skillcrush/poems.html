Some Poems

This is really mostly for typesetting practice and experimenting with fonts and fanciness.

  • Walt Whitman: "O you whom I often" — I don't think this one has a title, like most (all?) Whitman poems. From "Leaves of Grass".

    One of my favorite poems in English. I have a bunch of German poems that are closer to my heart, but also harder to share, so this was a good compromise. I love the rhythm of this one.

    O you whom I often and silently come where you are, that I may be with you; As I walk by your side, or sit near, or remain in the same room with you, Little you know the subtle electric fire that for your sake is playing within me.
    — Walt Whitman

  • William Shakespeare: Sonnet 145

    I so wanted to love all of Shakespeare's sonnets, but they never really moved me all that much. I liked this one though when I read it at age 16 or so, and I really like the "and saved my life" part.

    Those lips that Love's own hand did make
    Breathed forth the sound that said 'I hate'
    To me that languish'd for her sake;
    But when she saw my woeful state,
    Straight in her heart did mercy come,
    Chiding that tongue that ever sweet
    Was used in giving gentle doom,
    And taught it thus anew to greet:
    'I hate' she alter'd with an end,
    That follow'd it as gentle day
    Doth follow night, who like a fiend
    From heaven to hell is flown away;
    'I hate' from hate away she threw,
    And saved my life, saying 'not you.'
    — Shakespeare

  • Emily Dickinson: A Coffin—is a Small Domain

    From the Metaphysical Adventuress challenge!

    A Coffin—is a small Domain,
    Yet able to contain
    A Citizen of Paradise
    In its diminished Plane.

    A Grave—is a restricted Breadth—
    Yet ampler than the Sun—
    And all the Seas He populates
    And Lands He looks upon

    To Him who on its small Repose
    Bestows a single Friend—
    Circumference without Relief—
    Or Estimate—or End—

    — Emily Dickinson
